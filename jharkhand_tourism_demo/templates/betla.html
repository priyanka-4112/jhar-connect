About Betla National Park

Betla National Park is a beautiful and vast park that was one of the first in India to be declared a tiger reserve. It is home to a rich diversity of wildlife, including elephants, leopards, wild boar, and various species of deer and birds, all within a stunning landscape of lush forests and grasslands.

Key Sights & Activities

Jeep Safari

Embark on a thrilling jeep safari through the park's core areas to spot wildlife in their natural habitat.

Palamu Forts

Explore the historic ruins of two large forts built by the Chero kings, located deep inside the forest.

Elephant Rides

Experience a unique perspective of the forest and its inhabitants with a guided elephant ride.
